Athena Brain
Cognitive Foundation and Phase-1 Design Decisions
Version 0.1

Purpose
This document records the current design decisions regarding Athena Brain's cognitive architecture.
It is not an implementation document.
Its purpose is to define how Athena should perceive, learn and gradually build cognition.

Design Principle
Athena Brain is NOT another LLM.
LLM is only one component.
Athena's intelligence should not come from pretraining.
Instead, intelligence should emerge from continuous interaction with the world.
Therefore Phase-1 focuses on one goal:
Can an artificial intelligence gradually build concepts from experiences?

Question 1
Should Athena have its own vision model?
Current answer:
No.
But Athena should own its own cognition.
Vision and cognition are two completely different problems.

Human babies are not born knowing what a dog is.
However, they are born with a functioning visual system.
Their eyes detect edges, colors and motion.
Their brain learns meaning.
Therefore Athena should follow the same philosophy.

Question 2
What does "Don't memorize Wikipedia" actually mean?
The phrase
"Don't memorize Wikipedia"
can be misunderstood.
The real principle should be:
Knowledge should originate from evidence.
NOT from copied encyclopedia definitions.

Bad Example
Vision detects Apple.
Athena immediately imports:
Apple belongs to Rosaceae.
Originated in Central Asia.
Rich in Vitamin C.
...
This is merely copying external knowledge.
Athena has not actually learned anything.

New Design Principle
Athena does NOT learn answers.
Athena learns evidence.
Knowledge emerges from accumulated evidence.
This should become one of the fundamental principles of Athena Brain.

Why this separation matters
Without Raw Observation
Vision models directly inject concepts.
Athena becomes a database.

With Raw Observation
Vision provides perception.
Athena provides understanding.
The architecture becomes much closer to human cognition.

Phase-1 Conclusion
BabyBrain should NOT attempt to build a new vision model.
BabyBrain should verify one hypothesis:
Can concepts gradually emerge from evidence?
Everything in Phase-1 should serve this goal.
Nothing else should distract the architecture.
